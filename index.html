Jaryn Miguel

Pharmacy Technician

I first became interested in pharmacy after shadowing my mom's friend who was a pharmacist at a CVS pharmacy when I was in high school. It felt like the perfect field for me because I wanted a career that was medical related, but wasn't necessarily interested in hospital patient care/bedside care. I came from a family of nurses, so I didn't know much besides nursing. I also liked how there was both retail pharmacy, pharmaceutical research, and patient-care pharmacists. I'm still not too sure which I prefer, but I still like having the option.

Currently, I am a fourth year biochemistry major undergraduate student at UC Riverside, with a medical science concentration. I am taking additional courses that are necessary for me to be able to apply to pharmacy school. Among the science courses I've taken, I've found organic chemistry to be the most interesting, but also the most difficult.

As for work experience, I have been working at a Walgreens Pharmacy for over a year now, half of which has been spent as a PTCB certified, CA licensed pharmacy technician. I have gained experience in customer service, basic pharmaceutical knowledge, filling perscriptions, basic knowledge of insurance, speaking with doctors, and pharmacy laws. Although I find retail pharamcy very frustrating at times, I still like my job. I do want to experience hosptial pharmacy, and think I may like it more. As a pharmacy technician, I have also gotten licensed to give vaccines. I am also an intern at a CVS pharmacy, but it is not too different from Walgreens besides the fact that they have more advanced technology there to help with filling perscriptions.
